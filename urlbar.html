URL Bar

The most important aspect of your browser (because without it, it wouldn’t allow you to browse) is the URL bar. This is normally located at the top and allows you to type into it when you click on it. In this bar you can type the URL of the webpage you want to go to, youtube.com being an example. The browser will then request the webpage from YouTube’s servers and display the information it receives in turn. If you type in a webpage’s URL wrong, you might receive an error, or the browser will show you another of its very useful features and another which is also quite ubiquitous. Many browsers are capable of having Search Engines integrated into their URL bars.

But what does URL stand for? URL is an acronym which stands for Uniform Resource Locator. This name was coined by a man named Tim Berners-Lee, who also invented the World Wide Web, which you are currently experiencing by navigating my website. URLs are "Uniform" in that they all follow the same scheme, which is "scheme, authority, path, query, fragment." The URL locates resources in that it tells your computer where to look to find the information that you are requesting.
